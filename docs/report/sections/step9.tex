\subsubsection{Motion Sensor (PIR)}

A Passive Infrared (PIR) sensor is a type of motion detection device that operates without actively emitting energy. The term ``passive'' indicates that the sensor does not generate or radiate signals for detection purposes. Instead, it detects variations in infrared radiation naturally emitted or reflected by objects within its field of view. PIR sensors are compact, cost-effective, and energy-efficient, making them suitable for a wide range of applications. They are commonly used in residential, medical, and industrial environments for occupancy and motion detection.

\textbf{Sensor details:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Detection range: up to 7 meters
    \item Detection angle: 110 degrees
    \item Operating voltage: DC 4.5V--12V DC
    \item Output signal: 3.3V digital output
    \item Delay time: adjustable from 0.3 seconds to 5 minutes
    \item Operating temperature: -15$^\circ$C to +70$^\circ$C
    \item Sensitivity: Adjustable
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Working Principle of a PIR Motion Sensor}

\textbf{1. Infrared detection:}

A PIR sensor contains a pyroelectric element that detects changes in infrared radiation. Since all objects emit infrared energy, warmer objects produce a stronger signal.

\textbf{2. Sensor structure:}

The sensing element is divided into two sections that are arranged to balance each other under normal conditions. A lens is placed over the sensor to improve its sensing range and focus incoming infrared radiation.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\begin{minipage}[t]{0.45\textwidth}

\textbf{3. Idle state:}

When no movement is present, both sections receive approximately the same amount of infrared radiation. In this case, no differential signal is produced, so the output remains stable.

\textbf{4. Motion detection}

When a warm body moves across the sensor's field of view, one section receives more infrared radiation than the other. This creates a differential signal, which is then amplified and compared by the internal circuit.

\textbf{5. Output generation}

If the change in infrared radiation is significant, the comparator produces an output signal, indicating motion. When the object leaves the sensing area, the signal changes again, allowing the sensor to detect movement in real time.

\end{figure}

It is important to remember that the PIR sensor should be installed away from the Wi-Fi antenna to avoid affecting its performance, since Wi-Fi signals emit electromagnetic radiation that can interfere with the PIR sensor.

\subsubsection{Air Quality Sensor (IAQ)}

To manage and maintain clean indoor air, IAQ (Indoor Air Quality) sensors are employed to monitor key environmental pollutants and climate parameters. These sensors play a central role in air purification systems, HVAC control, building automation, and environmental safety.

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.45\textwidth]{9.1.2-air-quality-sensor.jpg}
\caption{Indoor air quality sensor module}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Key factors influencing IAQ:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Chemical pollutants (CO$_2$, VOCs, NO$_2$)
    \item Biological contaminants (mold, bacteria, allergens)
    \item Particulate matter (PM2.5, PM10)
    \item Environmental conditions (humidity, temperature, air pressure)
\end{itemize}

The IAQ sensor is essentially a multi-parameter electronic device that detects and quantifies various pollutants and environmental conditions within indoor spaces. These sensors may measure gases, particles, and climate-related parameters, then transmit the data to a monitoring or control system. The Smart Mosque system is designed to use a multi-sensor module rather than a single-sensor one.

\textbf{Common pollutants monitored by IAQ and their effects:}

\textbf{Working principles of IAQ sensors:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Sensing: Each sensor type uses a specific physical or chemical method to detect its target pollutant.
    \item Signal Processing: Detected signals are converted into electrical output (analog or digital).
    \item Calibration: Sensors are calibrated for accuracy, often using reference gases.
    \item Data Output: The processed data is sent via I2C, UART, Modbus, or wireless protocols (Wi-Fi, Zigbee, LoRa).
    \item Display and Analysis: Results are visualized on display or uploaded to the cloud for remote monitoring.
\end{enumerate}

Commonly, the output of an IAQ sensor can range from 0--50 (good), 51--100 (moderate), 101--150 (unhealthy for sensitive groups), and 151--200 (unhealthy).

A good quality sensor offers accurate readings, but like any sensor, IAQ devices come with several challenges:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Sensor drift and degradation over time
    \item Cross-sensitivity (e.g., VOC sensors reacting to humidity)
    \item Need for periodic calibration
    \item Data overload in multi-sensor networks
    \item False readings due to temperature/humidity interference
\end{itemize}

It is important to calibrate the sensor every 6--12 months to ensure good quality readings.

\subsubsection{Water Flow Sensor}

\begin{figure}[htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.45\textwidth]{9.1.3-water-flow-sensor.jpg}
\caption{Water flow sensor}
\end{figure}

Water flow sensors are installed at water sources or pipes to measure the flow rate of water and calculate the total amount of water flowing through the pipe. Flow rate is commonly measured in liters per hour or cubic meters. The sensor measures water flow in real time and provides pulse output that can easily connect to an MCU in simulation or real implementation.

\textbf{Working principles of a water flow sensor:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item A water flow sensor has a plastic body or valve that allows water to pass through it.
    \item Inside the sensor, a rotor and a Hall-effect sensor work together to detect and measure water flow.
    \item When water moves through the sensor, it rotates the rotor.
    \item The rotor's movement changes the rotation speed, which is converted into a pulse signal by the Hall-effect sensor.
    \item The pulse signal is then used to calculate the water flow rate.
    \item The main working principle is the Hall effect, where rotor motion induces a voltage difference measured by the sensor.
    \item This induced voltage represents flow movement inside the sensor.
    \item The flow value can then be displayed or processed by the controller.
\end{itemize}

Water flow sensors can be used with hot, cold, warm, clean, or dirty water depending on sensor type. In this project, clean water compatibility is sufficient. These sensors can be connected easily to microcontrollers such as the ESP32. The required supply voltage is typically between 5V and 18V DC.

\subsection{Output Devices (Actuators)}

\subsubsection{Smart Lights}

Smart lights are used as output devices to provide illumination in the mosque when needed. Their role is to support comfort and visibility during prayer times while reducing unnecessary energy usage during low occupancy periods.

\textbf{Working principle:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The system checks whether prayer time is active.
    \item It also checks whether a PIR sensor detects people in a specific area.
    \item If both conditions are true, lights turn ON automatically.
    \item If no people are detected, lights remain OFF even during the prayer window.
    \item After prayer time ends, lights turn OFF automatically.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{How it functions in the project:}

When the PIR sensor detects movement in a mosque section, the system interprets this as occupancy. If occupancy occurs during the prayer period, the lights activate automatically to provide illumination. When the area becomes empty, the lights turn OFF after a short delay to avoid unnecessary power consumption.

\subsubsection{Fans (AC Simulation)}

Fans and AC units maintain comfortable environmental conditions by responding to temperature, humidity, CO$_2$, and air-quality conditions.

\textbf{Working principle:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The IAQ sensor continuously measures temperature, humidity, and CO$_2$ levels.
    \item Ventilation fans activate when CO$_2$ exceeds thresholds, temperature exceeds 28$^\circ$C, or humidity exceeds 65\%.
    \item A dedicated AC unit activates when temperature exceeds 32$^\circ$C as a stronger cooling response.
    \item If environmental conditions remain acceptable, fans and AC stay OFF or operate at lower intensity.
    \item When conditions improve, the system reduces ventilation automatically.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{How it functions in the project:}

When the mosque becomes crowded, environmental readings indicate increased ventilation demand. The controller activates fans or AC accordingly to maintain comfort and airflow. Once temperature, humidity, and CO$_2$ return to acceptable levels, ventilation decreases automatically to reduce energy consumption.

\subsubsection{Solenoid Valve}

The solenoid valve controls the water supply in the ablution area using water flow sensor readings. Its role is to stop water waste when abnormal or continuous flow is detected.

\textbf{Working principle:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Water flow sensors continuously monitor water movement.
    \item If abnormal flow continues longer than expected, the controller interprets this as possible waste.
    \item The controller sends an electrical signal to the solenoid valve.
    \item The solenoid valve closes and stops water flow.
    \item The valve can reopen manually or automatically when normal conditions return.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{How it functions in the project:}

If the system detects water flowing longer than expected, it assumes a tap may have been left open or a leak is present. The controller activates the solenoid valve to stop the flow and prevent unnecessary waste.

\subsubsection{Smart Window}

The smart window improves airflow while reducing unnecessary cooling loss.

\textbf{Working principle:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item The system continuously monitors humidity and AC state.
    \item If the AC unit is ON, the smart window closes automatically.
    \item If the AC unit is OFF and humidity is high, the window opens for passive ventilation.
    \item Window operation updates dynamically according to environmental conditions.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{How it functions in the project:}

The smart window complements the ventilation system by providing natural airflow when mechanical cooling is not required, improving comfort and reducing unnecessary AC operation.

\subsubsection{Humidifier}

The humidifier maintains comfortable indoor humidity levels inside the mosque.

\textbf{Working principle:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Humidity sensors continuously monitor indoor moisture levels.
    \item If humidity falls below 30\%, the humidifier activates automatically.
    \item Once humidity returns to acceptable range, the humidifier deactivates automatically.
\end{itemize}

\textbf{How it functions in the project:}

The humidifier improves comfort for worshippers during dry environmental conditions and helps maintain stable indoor air quality.

\subsubsection{Street Lamp}

The street lamp provides lighting near the mosque entrance during periods of activity.

\textbf{Working principle:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item PIR motion sensors monitor activity near the entrance.
    \item When motion is detected, the controller activates the street lamp automatically.
    \item After inactivity, the street lamp turns OFF automatically.

\textbf{How it functions in the project:}

\subsection{Processing Unit}

The ESP32 microcontroller is selected as the processing unit because it is a powerful, low-cost, and low-power System-on-Chip (SoC) capable of supporting real-time IoT automation.

\begin{itemize}
    \item Wi-Fi connectivity: Built-in Wi-Fi supports network-based monitoring and automation.
    \item Multiple interfaces: Supports GPIO, I2C, UART, SPI, and PWM interfaces.
    \item Low power operation: Suitable for continuous operation throughout the day.
    \item Embedded IoT control: Capable of handling sensor reading and device actuation simultaneously.
    \item Practical for project scope: Well suited for managing occupancy, ventilation, lighting, and water-control systems together.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Communication Layer}

The communication layer enables reliable real-time data exchange between distributed sensors, the processing unit, and the dashboard.

\textbf{The system communication is implemented as follows:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Network Setup: A local Wi-Fi access point connects ESP32 nodes to the server.
    \item Protocol and Security: IEEE 802.11 Wi-Fi secured using WPA2-PSK encryption.
    \item Topology: Star topology simplifies management and expansion.
    \item Network Segmentation: IoT devices operate on a separate secured network segment.
    \item Real-World Implementation: MQTT or Zigbee-based solutions may be used in larger deployments.
    \item Simulation Representation: Cisco Packet Tracer models the system using MCU nodes connected to a Registration Server.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Integration Workflow}

The integration workflow follows the same process defined in the system process specification to ensure consistency between sensing, processing, and actuation stages.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Sensing Stage:} Sensors continuously monitor occupancy, environmental conditions, humidity, temperature, and water usage.
    
    \item \textbf{Data Transmission Stage:} Sensor readings are transmitted through the local Wi-Fi network to the processing unit.
    
    \item \textbf{Processing and Decision Stage:} The controller evaluates incoming data using predefined thresholds, prayer schedules, and occupancy logic.
    
    \item \textbf{Actuation Stage:} Control signals are sent to lighting, ventilation, windows, humidifiers, and water-control devices.
    
    \item \textbf{Monitoring and Feedback Stage:} Dashboard systems display current sensor readings, alerts, and device status while allowing administrator overrides.
\end{enumerate}

This workflow directly reflects the process flow defined earlier and ensures consistency across design and implementation.
