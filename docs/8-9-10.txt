8.4.3 Data Encryption 

Data transmitted within the system is protected using WPA2-PSK (Wi-Fi Protected Access 2 - Pre-Shared Key) encryption over the wireless network. WPA2-PSK is a Wi Fi security method that protects a network with a shared password and encrypts the data sent over it and does not require complex key management while still maintaining a strong level of confidentiality and integrity for transmitted data. This ensures that communication between sensors, the controller, and the dashboard is secured against unauthorized access.  

It works by using a shared password to authenticate devices and AES encryption to protect the Wi Fi traffic, with the password acting as the secret key that both devices and the access point use to connect securely. AES encryption is a symmetric encryption method that scrambles data into unreadable ciphertext using the same secret key for both encryption and decryption. By encrypting data at the network level, the system helps prevent attackers from intercepting or modifying sensitive information such as occupancy counts, water usage data, and device control commands. 

What the system does: 

    Secures wireless communication between sensors (motion, water flow, air quality) and the controller using WPA2-PSK.  

    Protects local dashboard traffic transmitted over the Wi‑Fi network. 

    Allows only authorized devices with the correct network credentials to join the network. 

    Provides a balance between security and performance, making it suitable for embedded IoT devices. 

8.4.4 Device Filtering 

Device filtering adds a basic access-control layer by allowing only approved IoT nodes to join the mosque network. In this project, MAC address filtering is used to create an allow list of trusted sensors, controllers, and dashboard devices, so any unregistered device is blocked from connecting. This is useful in a public environment because it reduces the chance of unknown devices joining the system and interfering with occupancy, water, or ventilation control. 

    Allow list only trusted devices: The router or access point can be configured so only the MAC addresses of authorized IoT nodes are permitted to connect. 

    Block unknown devices: Any device not on the list is denied network access, which helps keep the system limited to approved controllers and sensors. 

    Useful for IoT deployment: MAC filtering is practical in small, controlled networks like a mosque IoT setup because the number of devices is known and relatively stable. 

    Not a standalone security control: MAC addresses can be spoofed, so this method should be used together with WPA2-PSK and role-based access control rather than as the only protection. 

    Supports safer maintenance: When a new sensor or controller is added, its MAC address can be registered first, which gives the administrator a simple way to manage device enrollment.

9.1 Input devices (sensors) 

9.1.1 Motion Sensor (PIR) 

A Passive Infrared (PIR) sensor is a type of motion detection device that operates without actively emitting energy. The term “passive” indicates that the sensor does not generate or radiate signals for detection purposes. Instead, it detects variations in infrared radiation naturally emitted or reflected by objects within its field of view. PIR sensors are compact, cost-effective, and energy-efficient, making them suitable for a wide range of applications. They are commonly used in residential, medical, and industrial environments for occupancy and motion detection. 

 

Sensor details: 

    Detection range: up to 7 meters 

    Detection angle: 110 degrees 

    Operating voltage: DC 4.5V - 12V DC 

    Output signal: 3.3V digital output 

    Delay time: adjustable from 0.3 seconds to 5 minutes 

    Operating temperature: -15°C to +70°C 

    Sensitivity: Adjustable 

Working Principle of a PIR Motion Sensor: 

    Infrared detection: 
    A PIR sensor contains a pyroelectric element that detects changes in infrared radiation. Since all objects emit infrared energy, warmer objects produce a stronger signal. 

 

    Sensor structure: 
    The sensing element is divided into two sections that are arranged to balance each other under normal conditions. A lens is placed over the sensor to improve its sensing range and focus incoming infrared radiation. 

 

    Idle state: 
    When no movement is present, both sections receive approximately the same amount of infrared radiation. In this case, no differential signal is produced, so the output remains stable. 

    Motion detection 
    When a warm body moves across the sensor’s field of view, one section receives more infrared radiation than the other. This creates a differential signal, which is then amplified and compared by the internal circuit. 

    Output generation 
    If the change in infrared radiation is significant, the comparator produces an output signal, indicating motion. When the object leaves the sensing area, the signal changes again, allowing the sensor to detect movement in real time. 

 

It is important to remember that the PIR sensor should be installed away from the Wi-Fi antenna to avoid messing with its performance, since Wi-Fi signals emit electromagnetic radiation that can interfere with the PIR sensor.

9.1.3 Water Flow Sensor 

Water flow sensors are installed at the water source or pipes to measure the rate of flow of water and calculate the amount of water flowed through the pipe. Rate of flow of water is measured as liters per hour or cubic meters. It measures water flow in real time and gives pulse output that is easy to connect to an MCU in simulation or implementation.  

 

Working principles of a water flow sensor:  

    A water flow sensor has a plastic body or valve that allows water to pass through it. 

    Inside the sensor, a rotor and a Hall effect sensor work together to detect and measure water flow. 

    When water moves through the sensor, it rotates the rotor. 

    The rotor’s movement changes the speed of rotation, and this change is converted into a pulse signal by the Hall effect sensor. 

    The pulse signal is then used to calculate the water flow rate. 

    The main working principle of the sensor is the Hall effect, where rotor motion induces a voltage difference that is measured by the sensor. 

    This induced voltage is transverse to the electric current and represents the flow movement inside the sensor. 

    The flow value can then be displayed on an LCD screen or processed by a microcontroller. 

Water flow sensors can be used with hot, cold, warm, clean, or dirty water, depending on the sensor type. In our case, it should be enough for clean water of all temperatures. These sensors can be easily connected to microcontrollers such as Arduino. The sensor is usually installed at the water inlet or at the opening of the pipe. The required supply voltage is usually 5V to 18V DC.

9.2.3 Solenoid Valve  

The solenoid valve acts as an output device that controls the water supply in the ablution area based on the readings from the water flow sensor. Its role is to stop water waste when abnormal or continuous flow is detected, such as when a tap is left open or a leak occurs. 

Working principle: 

    The water flow sensor continuously measures the amount of water moving through the pipe. 

    If the system detects abnormal flow or flow that continues longer than expected, the controller interprets this as possible waste. 

    The controller then sends an electrical signal to the solenoid valve. 

    The solenoid valve closes and cuts off the water supply. 

    When normal conditions return, the valve can be opened again manually or by the control logic. 

How it functions in the project: 
When the sensor detects that water is flowing longer than it should, the system assumes that the tap may have been left open or that a leak is present. The controller then activates the solenoid valve to stop the flow and prevent waste. This helps the mosque conserve water while still allowing normal use when water demand is expected.
